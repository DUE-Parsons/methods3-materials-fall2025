Assignment 3

Now that you have some experience in QGIS, let's expand on that by making a few common types of maps: qualitative and quantitative maps.

Part 1: A Quantitative Thematic Map

As you did in Assignment 2b, focus on a small area within New York (or somewhere else where you can find similar data). Select a point data source such as:

  • 311 requests
  • car crashes
  • ...or some other points relevant to your interests/research.

Once you have your data:

  1. Filter the data down to a reasonable amount (for example, 311 requests of a particular type over the past month).
  2. Count the points in census tracts (2020 Census Tracts clipped to shoreline) using count points in polygons.
  3. Create a graduated style of the result, normalized by area.

Consider the colors you're using and the method you're using to classify the data. Consider the following:

  • Do this classification make sense to you?
  • Are outliers a quirk of the data? Or are they significant?
  • What story is your classification telling? What story would another classification tell?

It might help to refer to a few sources while thinking about this:

  • Lying with Maps
  • Telling the Truth
  • Making Choropleth Maps
  • Different Units, Different Patterns

Create a PDF of your map using a print layout. Include map elements as is useful (title, legend, and data source listing are a good start).

Share your work here including a short summary of your work. Explain what you created and how you did so. What did you have problems with? What did you enjoy? What would you like to know how to do next?
