attribute joins

"I have this spreadsheet I want to map..."

you need a file with relatable geographies

usually these are IDs of some sort

and a column in each file that relates the two

open the layer properties on the layer you want to add data to

this is almost always the spatial layer (shapefile or otherwise)

go to the joins tab and add a new one

join layer: the spreadsheet you're getting data from

join field: the field to join with from the spreadsheet

target field: the field to join with from the spatial layer

note that you can join any two layers that have attribute tables like this

we just joined a csv and a shp

but you can also join a xlsx and a geojson

or a shp and a shp

decennial census

The U.S. census counts every resident of the country every ten years on the year ending in zero. The US government has conducted a survey every ten years since 1790. The Census Bureau counts each resident where they live on April 1.
The Constitution mandates the enumeration to determine how to apportion the House of Representatives among the states. The census is not a count of citizens, it is a count of residents.

spatial joins

when you have layers that both have geographic features

Works best when you have:

  • points, lines, or smaller polygons that fall within them
